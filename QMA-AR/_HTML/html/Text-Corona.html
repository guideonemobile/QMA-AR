Corona – Queens

Situated just northwest of Flushing Meadows Corona Park, Corona is a New York City neighborhood that’s about as dynamic as they come. With the construction of the railroad in the mid-1800s, Corona transformed from a quaint rural village to a rapidly developed residential community. Today, Corona offers an eclectic mix of people and culture, with its population representing the kind of international diversity that has made Queens famous the world over. Aside from offering a delicious range of Latin American, Italian, and Asian cuisine, the neighborhood is also home to some of the city’s most beloved cultural museums such as the Queens Museum, the New York Hall of Science, and the Louis Armstrong House.

  • The neighborhood’s name was changed from West Flushing to Corona in 1872.
  • Louis Comfort Tiffany, founder of Tiffany Studios, built his stained-glass factory in Corona in 1893.
  • Louis Armstrong, American jazz legend, and his wife, Lucille, moved to Corona in 1943. They lived there until Louis’ death in 1971.
  • The Langston Hughes Community Library and Cultural Center of the Queens Library contains the largest circulating black heritage reference collection in New York City.
  • Corona is home to the city’s famous Lemon Ice King of Corona, started by Peter Benfaremo in 1944.
