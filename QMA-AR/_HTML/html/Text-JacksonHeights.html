Jackson Heights – Queens

From building the nation's first "garden apartment" complexes, to its community activism of the 1970s and 1980s, Jackson Heights has always been a neighborhood defined by its strong sense of community. Whether you're in the mood for South Asian cuisine or exhibitions by local artists, the neighborhood's rich cultural and culinary scene make it one of the most compelling destinations in Queens.

  • Named after John C. Jackson, who built Jackson Avenue, now Northern Boulevard.
  • In 1931, Jackson Heights resident Alfred Mosher Butts invented a word board game that would come to be known as Scrabble.
  • Radio personality, Howard Stern, and actress, Lucy Liu, were born in Jackson Heights. Actors John Leguizamo and Susan Sarandon grew up in the neighborhood.
  • Cooperative Apartments, also known as co-ops, were first introduced in Jackson Heights in the 1920s.
