Williamsburg – Brooklyn

From affluent rural retreat to industrial hub to world renowned center for the urban bohemian set, Williamsburg is a neighborhood with a presence that has spanned centuries. Few other New York City neighborhoods carry such historical and cultural cache and fewer still have garnered such sustained national and international attention. Today, Williamsburg stands as one of the cityâ€™s most sought after destinations for its culinary scene, commercial vibrancy and waterfront access.

  • Williamsburg is named after Colonel Jonathan Williams, who originally surveyed the land that is now the neighborhood today.
  • Designed by Leffert L. Buck, the Williamsburg Bridge was completed in 1903. The bridge was built to ease the congestion the Brooklyn Bridge.
  • In 1974, Boricua College opened a campus in Williamsburg, one of the first accredited bilingual colleges (Spanish and English) in New York City.
  • Actor/Comedian Mel Brooks was raised in Williamsburg.
  • Peter Luger Steak House, a neighborhood icon, has been serving steak dinners since 1887.
